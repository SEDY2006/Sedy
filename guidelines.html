Submission Guidelines

Essential information for preparing and submitting your research poster

Follow these steps to successfully participate in the Research Poster Competition 2025.

Complete the online registration form and pay the applicable fee to create your participant account.

Detailed Guidelines

Review the comprehensive guidelines below to ensure your submission meets all requirements.

Abstract Submission Guidelines

All participants must submit an abstract of their research before being invited to submit a full poster. Abstracts should provide a concise summary of the research work, highlighting its significance, methodology, findings, and conclusions.

Abstract Requirements:

  • Length: Maximum 300 words (excluding title, authors, and keywords)
  • Format: PDF file
  • Structure: Must include research title, author(s) name(s), institutional affiliation(s), introduction, methodology, results, conclusion, and 3-5 keywords
  • Font: Arial or Times New Roman, 12pt
  • Spacing: Single-spaced with standard margins
  • File naming convention: LastName_FirstName_Category_Abstract.pdf

Poster Design Guidelines

After abstract acceptance, participants must prepare their research posters according to the following guidelines. The poster should effectively communicate your research in a visually appealing and concise manner.

Poster Requirements:

  • Size: A0 (841mm x 1189mm / 33.1" x 46.8")
  • Orientation: Portrait only
  • Resolution: Minimum 300 DPI
  • File format: PDF
  • Maximum file size: 25MB
  • File naming convention: LastName_FirstName_Category_Poster.pdf

Poster Content:

  • Title: Clear and concise, font size 72pt or larger
  • Author(s) and affiliation(s): Include all contributors and their institutions
  • Sections: Include Introduction, Methodology, Results, Discussion, Conclusion, and References
  • Text: Use hierarchical structure with headings and subheadings
  • Graphics: High-quality images, charts, and graphs with clear captions
  • Tables: Keep simple and legible
  • References: Include key references in a standard format
  • QR code: Optional - can link to additional materials or contact information

Design Tips:

  • Use a clear, readable font (minimum 24pt for body text)
  • Maintain a consistent color scheme (3-4 colors maximum)
  • Balance text and visuals (aim for 40% text, 60% graphics)
  • Ensure logical flow of information (typically top to bottom, left to right)
  • Use white space effectively to avoid a cluttered appearance
  • Include institutional logo if applicable
  • Ensure high contrast between text and background for readability

Evaluation Criteria

All posters will be evaluated by a panel of judges comprising faculty members, industry experts, and research professionals. The evaluation will be based on the following criteria:

Research Quality

  • Originality and innovation
  • Methodological rigor
  • Significance of findings
  • Contribution to the field

Visual Presentation

  • Aesthetic appeal
  • Layout and organization
  • Effective use of visuals
  • Design coherence

Content Clarity

  • Clear communication of ideas
  • Logical flow and structure
  • Conciseness and readability
  • Grammar and spelling

Presenter's Knowledge

  • Understanding of the research
  • Response to questions
  • Engagement with audience
  • Professionalism

Scoring System:

Each criterion will be scored on a scale of 1-10, with the weighted percentages applied to calculate the final score. The maximum possible score is 100 points.

Presentation Day Guidelines

On the day of the competition, participants will present their posters to judges and attendees. Please follow these guidelines to ensure a smooth presentation experience:

Presentation Requirements:

  • Each presenter will be allocated a specific poster board and location
  • Presenters must arrive at least 30 minutes before their scheduled session
  • Prepare a 3-5 minute concise explanation of your research
  • Be prepared to answer questions from judges and attendees
  • Remain at your poster during the designated presentation times
  • Professional attire is recommended

Materials to Bring:

  • Printed copy of your poster (we'll provide mounting materials)
  • Business cards or contact information to share with interested parties
  • Optional: Small handouts summarizing your research (max A4 size)
  • Optional: QR code linking to supplementary materials or publications

Note:

Digital displays, projectors, or other electronic equipment are not permitted unless specifically approved in advance. If your research requires demonstration capabilities, please contact the organizing committee at least two weeks before the event.

Publication and Intellectual Property

By participating in the Research Poster Competition, you agree to the following terms regarding publication and intellectual property:

  • Abstracts and posters of award-winning entries may be published in the official conference proceedings
  • Selected high-quality submissions may be invited for publication in associated journals
  • You retain copyright of your work, but grant the organizers a non-exclusive license to display and publish your abstract and poster for promotional purposes
  • If your research is proprietary or contains confidential information, please ensure you have necessary permissions before submission
  • If your research is pending patent application, consult with your intellectual property office before submission
